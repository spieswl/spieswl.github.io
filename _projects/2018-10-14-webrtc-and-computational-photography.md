During my time spent in Northwestern University's **[Computational Photography Lab](http://compphotolab.northwestern.edu/)**, I traded my attention between the [3D Scanner described here]({{site.url}}/projects/2018/single-shot-structured-light-depth-scanning) and another fascinating project centering around **WebRTC**. WebRTC (with the RTC standing for Real-Time Communications) is a suite of APIs that enables simple browser-based capture and transfer of video and audio between devices. Several applications and products already leverage WebRTC for video conferencing, gaming, media sharing, and other social applications, so it has enjoyed growth and support since its introduction in 2013.

For our needs, I started looking at WebRTC in mid-2018 to determine if our lab could use such a technology as the basis for a general purpose data collection system.
